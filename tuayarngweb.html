ABOUT US

Our company has officially started cultivating and nurturing coffee, launching the project in July 2012 in Kongtun Village, Pakxong District, Champasak Province. Currently, the company has a total coffee plantation area of 150 hectares, with 130 hectares already bearing fruit. The plantation is expanded gradually each year, while also researching and addressing risks from diseases and pests, such as fungi, aphids, stem borers, coffee berry borers, and nutrient deficiencies. Additionally, natural disasters like drought, frost, and salt mist (which can kill coffee trees overnight) pose challenges. Despite these challenges, our company has successfully implemented preventive and corrective measures, ensuring a thriving coffee plantation. From 2018 to the present, there have been no tree losses due to diseases, pests, or natural disasters. The coffee plants continue to flourish and produce consistently increasing yields. In summary, as of today, the company has successfully completed the first milestone in establishing a high-quality coffee plantation with strong sustainability and a stable market.

From the practical implementation and lessons learned, it can be concluded that: The Bolaven Plateau region has a high potential for coffee production and can enhance its production capacity to be on par with other leading coffee-producing countries. The company’s experience has demonstrated that Arabica coffee can achieve yields of 15-20 tons per hectare, while Robusta coffee can reach 25-30 tons per hectare, consistently every year. If these targets are met, the revenue per hectare will not be less than 40-60 million Kip. When compared to production costs, it is evident that coffee cultivation in this region is profitable and sustainable.

To support the development of high-quality and high-yield coffee cultivation in the Bolaven Plateau, based on real-world experience, in 2020 a cooperative group of coffee producers and exporters from Paksong, Laongam, and Thateng districts, along with foreign investors, engaged in field studies, knowledge exchange, and confirmed that the region has high production potential, excellent quality, and a stable market.With this in mind, the company aims to promote and expand the production of high-quality coffee with stable market access in collaboration with local farmers who are committed to sustainable cultivation. This aligns with Laos' 2+3 agricultural policy. At the same time, the company urges the government to facilitate access to financial resources by providing low-interest loans for both farmers and businesses, ensuring that they have the necessary capital for production. Additionally, there is a need to improve organizational structures by establishing cooperatives or production groups, ensuring stronger producer networks, guaranteed production volumes, and consistent quality. Furthermore, expanding international coffee markets is crucial, especially by strengthening trade ties with neighboring countries.

OUR

GREEN BEANS


We take pride in producing some of the finest Arabica and Robusta coffees in the country, offering both washed and natural varieties. Our coffees are grown under the shade of lush forests on our own estates, nestled in a thriving ecosystem rich in biodiversity. Each coffee is hand-picked with care and processed delicately to unlock the inherent romance in every cup. From cultivation to harvest and processing, we prioritize sustainability at every step, ensuring that our coffees are not only exceptional in taste but also kind to the environment.

HOW OUR

GREEN BEANS OFFERING


COFFEE CHERRY

Coffee cherry from the coffee trees, these beans retain the juicy essence of coffee cherries, carrying a subtle aroma of ripe fruit blended with the earthy freshness of the soil. Each bean embodies the dedication of farmers who nurture them from seed to harvest, ensuring the highest quality. This is the beginning of a journey that transforms into the rich, aromatic coffee you love.

DRY PARCHMENT

Dry parchment coffee refers to coffee beans still encased in their protective parchment layer after the drying process. This natural shield helps preserve freshness, flavor, and aroma until the beans are hulled for roasting. Carefully processed and sun-dried to the perfect moisture level, dry parchment coffee ensures quality and consistency, maintaining the essence of its origin.

GREEN BEANS

Green coffee beans are unroasted and in their most natural state, preserving their raw, earthy aroma and essential characteristics. These beans are the foundation of every great cup, ready to be transformed through roasting into a variety of flavor profiles. Sought after for both specialty coffee roasting and health benefits, green beans represent the true essence of coffee before its final journey to your cup.

ROASTED COFFEE BEANS

Carefully roasted to perfection, these coffee beans unlock a rich symphony of flavors and aromas. The roasting process enhances their natural sweetness, balancing acidity and bitterness to create a smooth, full-bodied experience. From light to dark roasts, each profile offers unique notes—ranging from bright and fruity to bold and smoky. A perfect choice for coffee lovers seeking depth and character in every cup.

HOW OUR

GREEN BEANS ARE PROCESSED


MICROLOTS

These coffees are the best of what our coffee estates have to offer, with less than the top 0.1% of our total production. With highly selective picking and innovative processing methods, these coffees have graced some of the finest Specialty Coffee roasters across the world.

DRY MILLING

Our dry milling factory, located in Paksong in the Bolaven Plateau region, houses not only the mill but also a well-equipped quality laboratory and storage facility.

NATURAL

This is a natural method of processing green beans. Even though it appears to be simpler than wet milling, additional care is required for this method of processing. Select hand-picked ripe red cherries are dried with all the fruit layers intact. This results in the bean absorbing flavours of the fruit, leaving a heavy mouthfeel and lowered brightness.
